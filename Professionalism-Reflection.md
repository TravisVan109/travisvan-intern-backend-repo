## Understanding Professionalism at Focus Bear

Professionalism at Focus Bear means maintaining respectful communication, accountability, and collaboration while working in a relaxed and supportive environment. Even though the workplace culture is casual, employees are still expected to treat others with respect, communicate clearly, and behave responsibly.

A professional environment includes being kind to teammates, communicating effectively, avoiding toxic behaviour, and responding to feedback constructively.

## Professional vs. Unprofessional Behaviour

### Professional Behaviour
- Communicating clearly and respectfully in messages and meetings.
- Being punctual and prepared for meetings.
- Listening to teammates’ ideas and opinions.
- Giving constructive feedback that focuses on work, not the person.
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them.

### Unprofessional Behaviour
- Ignoring messages or failing to respond to teammates.
- Interrupting others or dominating conversations in meetings.
- Using rude or sarcastic language in communication.
- Blaming others for mistakes instead of solving problems.
- Making inappropriate jokes or discriminatory comments.


## Respectful Communication in Remote Teams

In remote teams, communication mainly happens through written messages and online meetings. Respectful communication includes:

- Using polite and clear language in messages.
- Being mindful of tone since text can easily be misunderstood.
- Responding to questions or requests within a reasonable time.
- Listening carefully during online meetings.
- Handling disagreements calmly and focusing on solutions.

For example, instead of saying “This is wrong,” it is better to say “I think we could improve this by trying another approach.”

## Giving and Receiving Feedback Professionally

When giving feedback, it is important to:
- Focus on the task or behaviour, not the person.
- Provide suggestions for improvement.
- Be respectful and constructive.

When receiving feedback, it is important to:
- Listen carefully and avoid reacting defensively.
- Ask questions if something is unclear.
- View feedback as an opportunity to learn and improve.

Assuming good intentions helps create a more supportive team environment.

## Experience with Good Teamwork

I have experienced effective teamwork in situations where team members communicated openly, respected each other's ideas, and worked together to solve problems. Good teamwork happens when everyone feels comfortable sharing ideas and when responsibilities are clearly divided among team members.

In these situations, trust, clear communication, and mutual respect made the team more productive and collaborative.

## Ensuring Clear and Respectful Communication

To make sure my communication is clear and respectful, I will:

- Write messages clearly and politely.
- Check my tone before sending messages.
- Respond to teammates in a timely manner.
- Be open to different perspectives and ideas.
- Avoid misunderstandings by asking for clarification when needed.

## Creating a Positive and Professional Work Environment

I can help create a positive and professional environment by:

- Being supportive and respectful to teammates.
- Encouraging collaboration and teamwork.
- Maintaining a positive attitude even when challenges occur.
- Treating everyone equally and respectfully.

A friendly and respectful environment helps everyone feel comfortable and motivated to work.


## Preparing for Meetings with Supervisors and Colleagues

To prepare for meetings effectively, I will:

- Review the meeting agenda or discussion topics beforehand.
- Prepare questions or updates related to my tasks.
- Take notes during meetings.
- Follow up on any action items after the meeting.

Being prepared helps meetings run more smoothly and productively.


## Being Proactive During My Internship

Being proactive means taking initiative and showing responsibility in my work. During my internship, I can be proactive by:

- Asking questions when I do not understand something.
- Updating my supervisor about my progress.
- Looking for ways to improve my work.
- Helping teammates when possible.

This shows engagement and willingness to learn.


## Following Up with Colleagues or Supervisors

If I need information or action from someone, I will first send a polite message or reminder. If there is no response after a reasonable amount of time, I can follow up again with a clear and respectful message.

If the issue is urgent or continues to block progress, I may escalate the issue by informing my supervisor so the team can address it quickly.


## Things I Will Avoid

To maintain professionalism, I will avoid:

- Using disrespectful or negative language.
- Ignoring messages or responsibilities.
- Sharing confidential company information.
- Engaging in bullying, discrimination, or toxic behaviour.
- Reacting emotionally to feedback or disagreements.


## Reporting Serious Issues

If I observe serious breaches of professional conduct, such as harassment, discrimination, or unethical behaviour, I understand that I should report the issue through the appropriate channels. This may include contacting management or using the CEO’s contact information provided in the onboarding materials.

Reporting serious issues helps maintain a safe and respectful workplace for everyone.


## Key Learning

Professionalism is not only about formal behaviour but also about respect, communication, accountability, and teamwork. Even in a relaxed workplace culture, maintaining professionalism helps create a positive environment where everyone can collaborate effectively and succeed.
