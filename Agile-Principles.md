# Agile Principles & Frameworks

# Research

## What Is Agile?
Agile is a way of working that focuses on flexibility, collaboration, and continuous improvement. Instead of planning everything upfront (like traditional project management), Agile uses small, iterative cycles that allow teams to adapt quickly as requirements or priorities change.

### How Agile Differs From Traditional Project Management
Traditional project management:
- Heavy planning upfront  
- Fixed scope and timelines  
- Sequential workflow (Waterfall)

Agile:
- Adaptive planning  
- Continuous feedback  
- Iterative delivery  
- Flexible scope  

This makes Agile more suitable for fast-changing, dynamic environments like software development.


## Core Values & Principles of Agile (Agile Manifesto)

### Core Values
1. **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools  
2. **Working software** over comprehensive documentation  
3. **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation  
4. **Responding to change** over following a plan  

### Key Principles (simplified)
- Deliver small, valuable increments frequently  
- Welcome changing requirements  
- Keep communication open and continuous  
- Maintain a sustainable pace of work  
- Reflect regularly and improve (retrospectives)  
- Build around motivated people  
- Keep things simple and avoid unnecessary work  

These values guide how Agile teams collaborate and deliver work.

## Scrum vs Kanban

### Scrum
- Works in **fixed-length sprints** (usually 1–2 weeks)  
- Has structured ceremonies: stand-ups, sprint planning, retrospectives  
- Team commits to a set amount of work per sprint  
- More structured and time-boxed  

### Kanban
- Focuses on **continuous flow** rather than sprints  
- No fixed iterations; task priority can change anytime  
- Uses a visual board with columns representing stages  
- Emphasizes limiting Work in Progress (WIP)  
- More flexible and adaptable  

### When Each Is Used
- **Scrum** works well when teams need predictability and structured cycles.  
- **Kanban** works well when priorities change frequently, tasks vary in size, or a continuous delivery model is required.

## Why Focus Bear Leans Toward Kanban
Focus Bear’s workflow changes quickly as new bugs, features, and user feedback come in. Kanban allows the team to:

- Re-prioritize work anytime  
- Deliver updates continuously  
- Avoid sprint commitments that may be disrupted  
- Maintain flexibility for a fast-moving startup environment  

The simplicity and adaptability of Kanban make it a better fit for Focus Bear’s style of working.

# Reflection

## Benefits and Challenges of Agile

### Benefits
- Clear communication and transparency  
- Ability to adapt quickly to change  
- Faster delivery of features  
- Continuous improvement through feedback  

### Challenges
- Requires consistent communication  
- Needs discipline to keep boards and tasks updated  
- Priorities change often, which can be confusing without structure  

However, the benefits usually outweigh the challenges, especially in software development.

## Summary: Scrum vs Kanban (In My Own Words)
Scrum uses **fixed sprints** with structured ceremonies and planned work for each sprint. Kanban is **more flexible**, using a continuous flow of tasks on a board with no fixed sprint cycle. Scrum is more structured, while Kanban allows work to be pulled and reprioritized anytime.

## Useful Agile Principle for My Work
The Agile principle I find most useful is:

**“Simplicity—the art of maximizing the amount of work not done.”**

This reminds me to:
- Focus on the essentials  
- Avoid over-engineering  
- Keep solutions clear and maintainable  
- Break tasks into smaller, simpler steps  

This principle helps me stay efficient and avoid unnecessary complexity in backend development.
