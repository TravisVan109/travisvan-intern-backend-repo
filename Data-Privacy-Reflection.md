# Data Privacy & Confidentiality Reflection

## Goal

Understand how to handle sensitive data responsibly and follow Focus Bear’s privacy and confidentiality policies.

# Research

## Key Takeaways from Focus Bear’s Privacy Policy

From reviewing the Focus Bear Privacy Policy, several important points stand out:

- Focus Bear collects personal data only for **specific and legitimate purposes**, such as providing services, managing subscriptions, and improving the app.
- The company follows **data protection laws such as the GDPR**, which ensures personal data is processed lawfully and securely.
- Personal data may include information like **email addresses, device information, habit data, survey responses, and subscription details**.
- Sensitive information such as **health-related or lifestyle data** may sometimes be collected through optional surveys or user-created habits.
- Focus Bear protects data using **security measures such as encryption, anonymisation, and trusted service providers**.
- Data is only shared with **authorised partners or service providers** who help operate the service.
- Users have rights over their data, including **access, correction, deletion, and the right to withdraw consent**.

## Types of Confidential Data at Focus Bear

Some examples of confidential or sensitive data include:

- User identification data (email address, phone number)
- Login and account information
- Habit data created by users within the app
- Survey responses and feedback (Academic & Professional Data, Social Datam, Profile Data and Special Category of Personal Data)
- Commercial and Marketing Data
- Device and technical information used for troubleshooting
- Payment-related information handled through secure payment providers
- Aggregate Data
- Internal company information such as systems, tools, and user analytics

This data must be handled carefully to protect user privacy and maintain trust.

## Best Practices for Handling Confidential Data

Best practices for protecting confidential data include:

- Only access data that is **necessary for your work tasks**.
- Never share sensitive information with **unauthorised people**.
- Use **secure systems and approved tools** to handle data.
- Avoid storing personal data on **personal devices or unsecured locations**.
- Ensure sensitive information is **encrypted or anonymised** when possible.
- Always follow company policies when handling user data.

These practices help reduce the risk of data breaches or misuse.

## Responding to a Suspected Data Breach

If a data breach or accidental disclosure happens, the correct response should include:

- Reporting the issue **immediately to the appropriate supervisor or security contact**.
- Avoiding further sharing or access to the affected data.
- Documenting what happened, including the time and type of data involved.
- Allowing the company to **investigate and manage the incident properly**.

Quick reporting is important to minimise damage and ensure compliance with privacy laws.

# Reflection

## Steps I Can Take to Handle Data Securely

To handle data responsibly in my daily work, I can:

- Follow company guidelines when accessing or handling user information.
- Use only **approved tools and systems** for work tasks.
- Avoid downloading or copying sensitive information unnecessarily.
- Log out of systems and keep my accounts secure.

These steps will help ensure that sensitive information is protected.

## Safe Storage, Sharing, and Disposal of Sensitive Information

Sensitive data should always be handled carefully:

- **Storage:** Keep data only in authorised systems or secure platforms.
- **Sharing:** Share information only with team members who need it for their work.
- **Disposal:** Delete or remove sensitive data when it is no longer needed and follow company policies for data retention.

This reduces the risk of information being accessed by unauthorised people.

## Common Mistakes That Cause Data Privacy Issues

Some common mistakes that can lead to privacy problems include:

- Sharing confidential data through insecure channels.
- Leaving accounts logged in on shared computers.
- Downloading sensitive files onto personal devices.
- Accidentally sending information to the wrong person.

These risks can be avoided by staying careful, following company policies, and double-checking before sharing information.

# Task Outcome

## Habit to Improve Data Security

One habit I will adopt is to **always double-check permissions and recipients before sharing any information** to ensure that only authorised people can access it.

## Key Learning

A key lesson from this activity is that **protecting user data is a critical responsibility**. Even small mistakes can lead to privacy risks, so it is important to follow security practices and handle all data carefully.
